S-JEPA: towards seamless cross-dataset transfer through dynamic spatial attention

2024-03-18 · Pierre Guetschel, Thomas Moreau, Michael Tangermann

Summary Highlights:

  • New EEG foundation model: Signal-JEPA introduces a novel spatial block masking strategy for EEG self-supervised learning
  • Achieves state-of-the-art performance on ERP and SSVEP BCI tasks, with spatial filtering proving critical for downstream accuracy
  • Longer pre-training examples (16s) significantly improve performance compared to shorter windows

Unique contribution: Signal-JEPA introduces a novel spatial block masking strategy for EEG self-supervised learning, enabling effective cross-dataset transfer.

Detailed summary

This exploratory study introduces Signal-JEPA, a Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture tailored for EEG signal representation. The key innovation is a domain-specific spatial block masking strategy that masks EEG channels within a radius of a randomly chosen central channel, enabling dynamic spatial filtering. The framework includes three novel downstream architectures for BCI tasks (motor imagery, ERP, SSVEP) and is evaluated on a 54-subject dataset. Results show that longer pre-training examples (16s) significantly improve downstream performance, while spatial filtering in the fine-tuning stage proves critical for accurate classification. The approach achieves state-of-the-art results on two of three BCI paradigms tested.
